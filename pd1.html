WHAT IS CONVALESCENT PLASMA?

Most people who recover from COVID-19 develop antibodies (proteins that the immune system produces in response to infection) to severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). Antibodies are found in plasma, the yellow liquid portion of blood. Plasma is collected from donors who have recovered from COVID-19 through a process called apheresis. The plasma is removed, while the rest of the blood components are returned into the donorâ€™s body.

WHO CAN DONATE CONVALESCENT PLASMA ?

One should be at least 17 years old and weigh 110 lbs. Additional weight requirements apply for donors age 18 or younger. One should be in good health. You generally feel well, even if you're being treated for a chronic condition. Have a prior, verified diagnosis of COVID-19, but are now symptom free.

PROCESS OF PLASMA DONATION

Donating plasma is similar to giving blood. A needle is placed into a vein in your arm. Plasma is collected through a process call plasmapheresis and is conducted in cycles that may take up to an hour. Whole blood is drawn. The plasma is separated from the red blood cells and other cellular components. These are returned to your body with sterile saline solution to help the body replace the plasma removed from the whole blood.
